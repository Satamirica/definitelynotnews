How we work with sources

The project draws on two types of sources. The first are classic academic works that laid the theoretical foundation of media research: McCombs and Shaw on agenda setting (1972), Entman on framing (1993), Propp on narrative (1928). These provide the conceptual tools for understanding media influence mechanisms.

The second type covers research specifically on disinformation and fake news: Ilchenko on fake news typology in electronic media, Wardle on classifying information disorder, Vosoughi, Roy and Aral on the speed of false news spread. These give tools for analysing the specific mechanisms by which fakes are produced and circulate.
