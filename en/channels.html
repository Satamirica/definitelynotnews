Who is The Royal Herald

The Royal Herald is the official outlet. It relies on institutional sources and conveys the position of those who hold recognised authority. This is not a propaganda mouthpiece – it is a newsroom that sincerely believes the official version is the most credible and sees no reason to challenge it.

The Herald does not lie. It publishes what authoritative sources tell it, closes stories when institutions declare them closed, and chooses for its headlines those aspects of events that confirm the system is working. This is honest journalism within a particular editorial logic.

How the Herald speaks

The Herald's language is confident and official, with references to archives and quotes from the chancellery. It never asks open questions – it states. Its stories end with a conclusion, not a question.

Characteristic phrases
"According to the royal chancellery, the situation is under control."
"The Herald confirms: the decree has come into force. The matter is closed."
"The Forest Guard assures us: such incidents are the exception, not the rule."
"The incident is resolved. The agenda is closed."

When the Herald makes mistakes

In the fake news block, the Herald also makes mistakes – publishing a photograph without source verification, accepting indirect testimony as confirmation of a direct claim. When this comes to light, the Herald acknowledges the error and explains what went wrong. That is part of its character: institutional accountability.

Who is The First Crier

The First Crier is the independent outlet that looks for what was left out of the official version. It is not an opposition pamphlet or an exposé machine – it is a newsroom with a different angle, one that believes the full picture requires other voices too.

The Crier asks questions not because it suspects the authorities of deception, but because that is its journalistic task – to look wider than official sources. This is also honest journalism – within a different editorial logic.

How the Crier speaks

The Crier's language is lively, with specific details and the voices of real people. Its stories end with an open question or an observation the viewer must complete themselves. It does not close stories – it opens them.

Characteristic phrases
"We managed to find out: neighbours had been warning about this since last autumn."
"The fox has not contacted the editorial team. The fate of the Bun has not been officially confirmed."
"The question of who is on whose territory here is somewhat more complicated than it appears."
"The First Crier continues to investigate."

When the Crier makes mistakes

In the fake news block it is the Crier that first publishes unverified information about the Round Bun – rushing, relying on a single source, presenting a version as a fact. This is a crucial part of the project: independence and critical perspective do not protect against mistakes. Good editorial practice is for everyone.
